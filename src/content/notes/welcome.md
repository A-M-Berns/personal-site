This site is a landing page for my independent research and a home for cross-posts of my LessWrong writings. It's still under construction.

Sidenotes work by writing standard Markdown footnote syntax.[^1] On wide screens the footnote appears in the right margin, beside the paragraph it belongs to; on narrow screens it collapses and can be toggled inline.

You can put anything in a sidenote, including links and light formatting.[^tufte] Multiple sidenotes in a single paragraph also work.[^stack] And margin notes (no number) are available too.[^margin]

[^1]: This is a sidenote. It was written as `[^1]: ...` at the bottom of the Markdown file.

[^tufte]: Inspired by Edward Tufte's [book design](https://edwardtufte.github.io/tufte-css/) and Andy Matuschak's [notes site](https://andymatuschak.org).

[^stack]: They can overlap on long paragraphs — polishing that is a later project.

[^margin]: {-} Prefix a footnote definition with `{-}` to render it as an unnumbered margin note.
